Before I sold scripts to every major studio in Hollywood, I couldn't figure out why my scripts weren't working.

I'd rewrite the same scenes over and over. Get vague feedback like "something's off." But never know what was actually broken.

Then a mentor taught me to diagnose story problems like a script doctor. That changed everything.

$2.5 billion in box office later (Shrek 2, Jimmy Neutron, Rugrats, Disenchanted), I'm teaching what I learned.

The Corkboard

My daughter just got into NYU. In her college essay, she wrote about my corkboard.

For years, she watched me pin salmon-colored index cards—scenes, beats, rewrites—slowly figuring out what was broken.

She wrote: "Creativity wasn't only spontaneous—it required structure and dedication."

I didn't know she was watching. I was just trying to survive as a writer.

But she saw something worth writing about. A process. A practice. A way of working that combined inspiration with discipline.

That's what I'm building here. A place where writers develop that same practice together.
